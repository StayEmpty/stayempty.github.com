A bit of history

Domain51 started in the spring of 1999 in El Paso, Texas. Travis started work on building out his first website for a client, and during the summer of that year decided he wanted to stick with it.

Since that time, Travis has focused on building quality programs. He's honed his skills on numerous open-source projects, contributing ideas and code to projects in PHP, Python, and Ruby, always trying to stay abreast of all of the technologies that affect the web.

After consulting for 5 years, Travis decided to join the "real" world and go to work for others. After stints at several open-source startups and a large social networking site, Travis decided he needed to strike out on his own and work on projects that made the world a better place.

Now, based in downtown Lawrence, Kansas, Domain51 has reopened with a focus on web application for non-profits and activism. That's what the slogan means. We're not in the trenches, but we're supplying ammo to those who are getting their hands dirty. We're hacking a better world.

Why we do it

We're passionate about excellent code. We're passionate about making the world a better place. We're passionate about everything we do.

We're the guys who already do this free. We contribute to open source, we spend our weekends knocking out code for ourselves, we learn new languages for nothing more than the challenge. We're excited about programming because that's our passion. That we get paid to do is what makes this a great gig.

The deal with the name

From Travis:

I get asked this every time I hand out a card. "What's the deal with the name Domain51?" It's simple. I was looking for a unique name, and had probably just watched something on the History Channel or Discover talking about Area 51. I put two and two together and ended up with Domain51 and the name stuck.
